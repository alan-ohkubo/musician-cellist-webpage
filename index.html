Welcome

My name is Alan, and I am a cellist. Thank you for visiting my webpage.

Biography

Cellist, Alan Ohkubo recently completed a fellowship at the New World Symphony in Miami Beach, Florida. He was a member of the New Haven Symphony Orchestra and served as Principal Cello of the Eastern Connecticut Symphony Orchestra, where he appeared as soloist in Dvořák's cello concerto. The New London Day praised Alan for "technique with a swaggering panache, retaining musicality...robust fullness from lower register...to the top."

Alan completed an Artist Diploma and a Master of Music degree at the Yale University School of Music under the tutelage of Aldo Parisot. Prior to Yale, Alan studied with Janos Starker at Indiana University as a Barbara and David Jacobs Scholar.

An avid chamber musician, Alan has performed alongside artists such as David Shifrin, Lawrence Dutton, Cho-Liang Lin, Daniel Ching, and Ko Iwasaki, among others. He has also appeared in solo and chamber recitals in Tokyo and New York City on Charlotte White's Salon de Virtuosi concert series. Alan took part in a series of concerts in Carnegie Hall while participating in the New York String Orchestra Seminar. In recent summers, he has attended Music Masters Course Japan, an intensive chamber music seminar held in Yokohama.

Alan is also a passionate teacher. He teaches privately and is a faculty member at Porterville Strings, a summer String workshop in Porterville, California. Alan taught undergraduate cello and chamber music at Yale University as a teaching assistant.
